Equation of straight Lines

You should be aware that the general equation of a straight line graph is defined as \[ y=mx+C\] Where \( m \) is named the gradient (or slope) of the equation & \( C \) is the "y-intercept" (this is where it cuts the y-axis). \( m \) can be found by taking any 2 sets of point on the line & calculating the ratio in the difference between y & x coordinates in other words : measuring its change in height vs its change in width \[ m = \frac{y_2-y_1}{x_2-x_1} \] You will often be asked to find the equation of a straight line either i) from a picture of its graph or ii) given 2 sets of points which lie on it Click here to practice some example
